It is expected that you gain an understanding of the connection graphs of position, velocity, and acceleration have with each other. You will also be analyzing what the shape of a particular graph tells you about its motion.

Slope

The tangent of a line tells you the instantaneous slope at any given point. It is useful in analyzing graphs of motion. You will explore what the tangent, or slope, of two different types of graphs will show you.

    Slope of Position

  1. Make sure you can see both the position and velocity graphs
  2. Under 'Analyze' choose 'Tangent'
  3. Select a point on the position graph and record the slope at that point under slope-velocity 1
  4. Record the value of the velocity at that point as well. NOT THE SLOPE of the velocity, just the velocity
  5. Find a slope-velocity pair for one other point on the position graph

    6. Slope of Velocity

  6. Click on 'Position' next to the top graph and change it to 'Velocity'
  7. Click on 'Velocity' next to the bottom graph and change it to 'Acceleration'
  8. Select 'Tangent' again from the 'Analyze' menu
  9. Select a point on the velocity graph and record the slope at that point under slope-accel 1
  10. Recorrd the value of the Acceleration at that point as well. NOT THE SLOPE of the acceleration, just the acceleration.
  11. Find a slope-acceleration pair for one other point on the velocity graph

Area

The area under a slope of a graph is defined as the region between the plot and the x-axis. If the plot is above the x-axis, then the area is considered to be positive. If the area is below the x-axis then the area is considered to be negative. In calculus we use the integral to find the area under a curve. Area is useful in analyzing graphs of motion. You will explore the integral, or area, of two different types of graphs.

    Area of Acceleration

  1. Zoom in so that you can only see a few bounces on your screen
  2. Highlight an area of the velocity graph that is a continuous slope
  3. Under the 'Analyze' menu, select 'Integral' and choose 'Acceleration'
  4. Record the integral of the acceleration that you have selected
  5. Under the 'Analyze' menu, select 'Statistics' and choose 'Velocity'
  6. Record the maximum and minimum velocities over the highlighted area
  7. Find the change in velocity using the idea that ΔV = Vf - Vi
    • In this case you will use max velocity as Vf and min velocity as Vi
  8. Find one more area-ΔV pair for your acceleration graph

    9. Area of Velocity

  9. Change the bottom graph to velocity and the top graph to position
  10. Zoom in so that you can only see a few bounces on your screen
  11. Highlight and area of the position graph where the graph is constantly sloping up
  12. Under the 'Analyze' menu, select 'Integral' and choose 'Velocity'
  13. Record the integral of the velocity that you have selected
  14. Under the 'Analyze' menu, select 'Statistics' and choose 'Position'
  15. Record the maximum and minimum positions over the highlighted area
  16. Find the change in position using the idea that Δs = sf - si
    • In this case you will use max position as sf and min position as si
  17. Find one more area-Δs pair for your velocity graph.
