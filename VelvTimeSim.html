Velocity Vs. Time Graphs

Interactive Instructions
  1. Label on your paper the time interval(s) where the object is stationary.
  2. Label on your paper the time interval(s) where the object is moving at constant velocity.
  3. Label on your paper the time interval(s) that the object is accelerating in the positive direction.
  4. Label on your paper the time interval(s) that the object is accelerating in the negative direction.
  5. On your paper, determine the area under the curve of the velocity vs. time graph during the first 10 seconds.
  6. What is the displacement or change in position of the object during the first 10 seconds?
  7. Use the time sliders to check your answer.
  8. On your paper, determine the area under the curve of the velocity vs. time graph during the time interval from 2 to 4 seconds.
  9. What is the displacement of the object during the time interval from 2 to 4 seconds?
  10. Use the time sliders to check your answer.
  11. Is it possible to determine exactly where the object is with just the information from the previous question? Why or why not?
  12. Determine the exact position of the object after 4 seconds.
  13. Use the time sliders to check your answer.
