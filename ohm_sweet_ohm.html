Top
  1. Make a prediction about the relationship between the Voltage (ΔV) and the Current (I)
  2. Make a data table with columns for Potential (ΔV) in volts and Current (I) in amps, include the units in the heading. You will have 5 data points.
  3. Plug the Ammeter into ch 2 and the voltmeter into ch 1 like in the picture.
  4. Push on the button at the back of the volt probe to show the metal hook.
  5. Connect the black and red probes together to zero the volt meter. On the LabQuest, zero the voltmeter while they are connected as seen in the picture.
  6. Connect the voltmeter probes to the lone resistor as seen in the picture.
  7. Connect the pink wires to each side of the Ammeter as seen in the photo.
  8. Connect each of the pink wires to the breadboard as seen in the photo.
  9. Zero the ammeter from the LabQuest.
  10. Connect the battery to the battery holder and connect the battery holder to the breadboard as seen in the photo.
  11. Measure the voltage across the resistor in volts. Record the value.
  12. Measure the current through the circuit in amps. Record the value.
  13. Remove one of the brown-black-red resistors.
  14. Repeat the last 3 steps until there is only one brown-black-red resistor left.
Top
  1. Use graphical analysis to plot the data
    1. Plot the Current (I) in the x-axis
    2. Plot the Voltage (ΔV) in the y-axis
  2. Find the slope of the line using the curve fit tool.
  3. Record the slope of the line with your data table.
