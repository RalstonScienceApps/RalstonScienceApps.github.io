Position Vs. Time Graphs

Interactive Instructions
  1. Label on your paper the time interval(s) where the object is stationary.
  2. Use the "time" slider on the interactive to determine if you are correct.
  3. Label on your paper the time interval(s) where the object is moving at constant velocity.
  4. Use the "time" slider on the interactive to determine if you are correct.
  5. Label on your paper the time interval(s) that the object is accelerating in the positive direction.
  6. Use the "time" slider on the interactive to determine if you are correct.
    • Remember that the slope of the line is the velocity.
    • Show that the velocity changes by recording two velocities within the time interval on your paper.
  7. Label on your paper the time interval(s) that the object is accelerating in the negative direction.
  8. Use the "time" slider on the interactive to determine if you are correct.
    • Remember that the slope of the line is the velocity
    • Show that the velocity changes by recording two velocities within the time interval on your paper.
  9. Using the idea of the "Tangent Line", determine the velocity of the object at 1.5 seconds, 5.0 seconds, and 9.0 seconds.
  10. Use the "time" slider on the interactive to check your answer.
  11. Determine the percent error between your measurement and the accepted value.
